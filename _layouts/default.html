Narrative

Framed for his captain’s murder, Date Nasanube (or Senji Tome) must escape the village and turn the people against General Oda Nobunaga by burning the grain storage.

The environment tells its own story—a prisoner, Ushi Maki Ramen, has been caught smuggling goods and offers a secret escape in exchange for help. Meanwhile, guards misuse confiscated wares, exposing the corruption within.

Players can choose their approach: sneak through hidden paths or fight their way to freedom.

Guiding the Player

The level naturally guides players using lighting, elevation, and composition. From a high vantage point, players can scan the open gatehouse and plan their route.

A large window casts god rays onto the main gate, subtly marking the objective, while brightly lit paths contrast with darker side rooms to steer movement.

Bright cloth on vaultable windows clearly signals traversal points. Verticality creates a natural difficulty curve—starting above grants control, but descending increases tension.

Blockout & Reiterations

The level started as a hand-drawn layout followed by inspiration research for both the exterior and interior of the gatehouse. Using ProBuilder in Unity, I built the initial blockout and tested it with mentors and peers.

The biggest issue was excessive symmetry, which made movement predictable. To fix this, I added structural support beams on the lower floor and a new commander’s study on the upper floor, breaking up the uniformity. I also refined lighting and environmental cues to better guide the player.

These changes made the level more engaging and challenging, maintaining the advantage of high ground while ensuring movement felt dynamic and varied.

Reflections

This project taught me how to use ProBuilder and similar tools as well as lighting techniques and player guidance in stealth-based level design. I learned the importance of giving players a vantage point early on, allowing them to plan their route strategically.

The biggest challenge was grasping the fundamentals of level design—from planning the layout to using new tools like ProBuilder. At first, it was frustrating, but overcoming those struggles helped me understand how to approach the early phases of level creation more effectively.

If I had more time, I would refine color usage, narrative elements, and lighting adjustments to further enhance the atmosphere. I would also implement audio and music, as I believe sound design could elevate the stealth experience by reinforcing tension and immersion.
